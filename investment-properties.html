Financing Built for Investors

Investment property financing is fundamentally different from owner-occupied mortgage qualification. Lenders evaluate rental income, vacancy rates, debt service coverage ratios, and the overall strength of your existing portfolio — not just your personal income and credit score. Getting this wrong costs you the deal; getting it right unlocks significant leverage.

At Moneta Mortgages Inc., our brokers work exclusively on investment files daily. We know which lenders use 80% of gross rental income in their calculations, which accept market rents for newly acquired properties, and which offer portfolio programs for clients with multiple properties. This expertise is your competitive advantage.

The BRRRR Strategy — Financed Correctly

Buy, Renovate, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — the BRRRR strategy is one of the most effective wealth-building approaches available to Canadian real estate investors. It works by forcing equity through renovation, then pulling that equity back out via a cash-out refinance to fund the next acquisition.

Executing BRRRR correctly depends entirely on the financing structure. We advise clients on:

Acquisition Financing — Often purchased with cash or private bridge lending to allow for quick closing and renovation without mortgage delays.

Renovation Bridge Loans — Short-term financing that covers both purchase and renovation costs while the property is being improved.

Post-Renovation Refinance — Refinancing to the improved appraised value to extract equity; we time this with appraisal best practices.

Seasoning Period Advice — Understanding which lenders have no seasoning requirements vs. 6-to-12-month waiting periods before refinance.

Key Investment Mortgage Terms Explained

BRRRR Strategy in Action

Understanding these metrics is essential to evaluating any investment property opportunity:

Minimum Down Payment
20% minimum for non-owner-occupied investment properties. CMHC insurance is not available for pure rental properties — conventional financing only.
Rental Offset
Most lenders add 50%–80% of the subject property's rental income to your qualifying income. We target lenders allowing the maximum offset to improve your qualification.
Stress Test (B-20)
Investment properties are still subject to the federal stress test — qualification at contract rate + 2% or 5.25%, whichever is higher. We model this before you make an offer.
Maximum Amortization
Up to 30 years for investment properties with conventional financing. Longer amortization improves monthly cash flow, though total interest paid increases.
Debt Service Coverage
Net rental income ÷ mortgage payment. Commercial lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.10–1.25x. We structure your deal to meet these thresholds.

Multi-Property Portfolio Strategy

Once you own more than four investment properties, most traditional "A" lenders will no longer lend to you under residential mortgage rules. This is where many investors stall — but it doesn't have to be the end. Our brokers specialize in the transition to portfolio and commercial lenders who underwrite on asset performance rather than personal income thresholds.

We work with you to structure your portfolio across lenders strategically — avoiding concentration risk, maintaining flexibility, and preserving your ability to continue growing. This includes advising on corporate ownership structures (holding companies, bare trusts) in coordination with your accountant and legal team.
